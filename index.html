Prerequisites & Preparation
Everything to arrange before departure
📄
Mandatory Documents
  • Passport valid 6 months beyond return date
  • ESTA — mandatory for EU citizens. Apply at esta.cbp.dhs.gov at least 72h before departure. Valid 2 years, ~$21
  • International Driving Permit — not mandatory but strongly recommended alongside your national license
  • International credit card (Visa or Mastercard) required for car rental deposit
  • Copies of all documents (paper + email to yourself)
⚠ ESTA must be approved before purchasing flights if not already done.
🛡️
Recommended Insurance
  • Trip cancellation / interruption — subscribe when booking flights
  • International medical insurance — US healthcare is extremely expensive. Check Visa Premier / Mastercard Gold card coverage first
  • Emergency repatriation — usually included in medical insurance. Essential
  • Adventure sports coverage — via ferrata and downhill MTB are often excluded from standard policies
  • Rental car insurance — decline agency offer if credit card already covers it
💉
Health & Vaccinations
  • Required vaccines — none required to enter the US from the EU
  • Recommended — update DTP and hepatitis B. Consult a travel clinic at least 4 weeks before departure
  • Altitude — notify your doctor if cardiac history. Prefer acetaminophen over ibuprofen at altitude
  • First aid kit — antihistamines, SPF50+, blister pads, rehydration salts, personal medications
  • Mustard allergy — carry a card in English: "I have a mustard allergy."
📱
Connectivity & Apps
  • Roaming or US eSIM — Airalo / Holafly ~$20–30, unlimited data for 15 days
  • Google Maps offline — download Colorado before departure (no signal in mountains)
  • recreation.gov — create account for RMNP Timed Entry & Maroon Bells shuttle
  • Uber / Lyft — activate account before departure
  • Currency — $1 ≈ €0.92 (verify before departure)
🎒
Essential Gear
  • Hiking boots — ankle support required for via ferrata
  • Layering system — temperatures range from 23°F (-5°C) (Pikes Peak summit) to 95°F (35°C) (Sand Dunes)
  • Water bottle 1.5L+ per person (refill at visitor centers)
  • Harness & helmet — provided on-site at Royal Gorge Via Ferrata
  • Power adapter — US Type A/B (110V, 60Hz)
  • Sunscreen SPF50+ — intense UV at altitude
💰
Budget & Payments
  • Cash — keep ~$100 for parking and small vendors
  • Tips — 15–20% at restaurants, 10–15% taxi. Culturally non-optional
  • Sales tax — prices displayed are pre-tax. Add ~8–10%
  • America the Beautiful Pass — ~$80, covers RMNP + Black Canyon + Sand Dunes
  • car rental — budget ~$600–900 for 5 days. Book via Costco Travel or AutoEurope

Practical Notes
Key reminders for a smooth trip
Altitude & acclimatization
Denver: 5,280 ft · Breckenridge: 9,600 ft · Trail Ridge Road: 14,259 ft. Drink 3L+ water/day, avoid alcohol first two days, watch for headaches.
Priority reservations
RMNP Timed Entry (recreation.gov), Maroon Bells shuttle, Royal Gorge Via Ferrata, Red Rocks concert, Pikes Peak Cog Railway. Book as soon as dates confirmed.
Mustard allergy
Always say: "I have a mustard allergy." Safer cuisines: Mexican, Japanese, American BBQ. Avoid standard dressings and pre-made sandwiches.
Mountain weather
Afternoon thunderstorms nearly daily in Jul–Aug. Start hikes before 1 PM. Bring a windbreaker even in full summer. Summit temps below 50°F (10°C) year-round.
Independence Pass road
Breckenridge→Aspen via CO-82 · 12,095 ft · Open mid-May to mid-Nov. Check conditions before driving. Alternative via US-50 adds ~2.5 hours.
Great Sand Dunes
Sand surface exceeds 150°F (65°C) after 1 PM. Arrive before 10 AM. Bring 3L+ water per person, SPF50+, hat. Sandboard rental ~$20 at park entrance.
